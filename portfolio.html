Philosophy

How I think about quality

01 —

Risk first, coverage second

Not everything needs to be tested equally. I map risk to business impact and prioritize ruthlessly — protecting what matters most rather than chasing 100% coverage for its own sake.

02 —

Shift left before shifting fast

Defects found in requirements cost a fraction of those found post-release. I push quality as early into the SDLC as possible — sitting in design reviews, not just sprint demos.

03 —

AI needs a different kind of testing

AI systems don't have deterministic outputs. Quality here means evaluating probability distributions, semantic coherence, and failure modes — not just pass/fail assertions.

04 —

Teams are leverage

The best QA infrastructure I can build is people who don't need me to make decisions. I invest heavily in documentation, playbooks, and autonomy frameworks that scale without me.

05 —

Metrics should tell a story

Pass rates, defect density, and cycle time are lagging indicators. I track them — but I also ask what they're hiding and build leading indicators that give teams earlier signal.

06 —

Quality is the whole team's job

A QA team that gates quality at the end is already failing. My goal is always to make quality a shared responsibility — working with engineers and PMs who test alongside me.
